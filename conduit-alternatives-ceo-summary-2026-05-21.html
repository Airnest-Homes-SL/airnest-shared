§01 The Call

Pilot two vendors. Pay for OTA inbox only where we actually need it.

Recommendation
Split the portfolio into two messaging buckets: Hotel Masanet & Emeté on Mews-native (no third-party cost), and everything else (Bandama + all VR buildings + scattered VRs) on a single OTA-inbox vendor. Pilot Duve and Akia side-by-side on Poem + Bandama for 60 days, get real quotes, then commit.

The real OTA-inbox-paying unit count is ~89, not 137. Once we exclude the two hotels that don't take Airbnb or Booking.com, and we count Mews-side spaces (not Guesty umbrella listings), the per-unit math changes. Conduit's flat €900/mo is overkill for this shape; a per-unit vendor sized to ~89 units lands somewhere in the €450–700/mo range — saving €200–450/mo, not the €585/mo v1 claimed.

§02 What Yesterday's Brief Got Wrong

Three errors. All material.

Yesterday's recommendation (HelloShift, ~€315/mo) was wrong. Four research documents reconciled, plus Mews-marketplace and live-pricing checks, surface three structural mistakes I want to correct openly before anything is signed.

  1. HelloShift can't receive Airbnb messages. Nor can Bookboost, HiJiffy, or Chatlyn.
    On 30 September 2023 Airbnb shut down email-alias relay — the back-channel most "unified inbox" vendors used to receive Airbnb chat. Only vendors with a direct Airbnb API integration still pull inbound Airbnb messages today: Duve, Enso Connect, Trengo (Airbnb only), Akia. HelloShift was disqualified by one of the four research docs explicitly; the other three missed this and recommended it anyway.
  2. Hotels and VRs were lumped at one price.
    All four research docs computed "€X × 137 listings." But the channel data tells a different story: Hotel Masanet/Emeté is on Expedia + Google only (no Airbnb, no Booking.com via this account), so it doesn't need a unified OTA inbox at all — Mews native covers it. Bandama Hotel, by contrast, is on every OTA. The two hotels deserve different treatment.
  3. The list-price math doesn't survive contact with vendor pricing pages.
    Duve's actual pricing page shows OTA messaging is an add-on across all tiers — the €5/room baseline cited in the docs is the entry plan without OTA inbox. Enso Connect, Akia, and Chatlyn have all moved to "request a quote" — no public list price. Real cost for any of these will only be knowable through a sales conversation, which is why the recommendation below is a two-vendor pilot, not a single-vendor commit.

§06 The Ask

Three decisions, this week.

  1. Approve the split: Mews-native for Masanet & Emeté, third-party for everything else.
    If you agree the channel data above justifies the split, the two hotels need no further messaging procurement — they're done. That's one less decision and one less line on the bill.
  2. Authorise a 60-day dual pilot: Duve + Akia, on Poem + Bandama Hotel.
    Same two properties, same two months, side-by-side. Measure: OTA-message coverage (all three channels actually arriving), Canarias-Spanish reply quality, staff adoption, response latency vs the Conduit baseline. Both vendors run free trials or low-commit pilots; total spend < €1,500. At day 60, kill the loser and roll the winner to the rest of Bucket B.
  3. Hold Conduit's notice until day 45 of the pilot.
    Don't cancel Conduit before the pilot proves out. At day 45 we'll know which vendor wins; we give Conduit 30-day notice then, ending around day 75 of the pilot. If both pilots fail, we stay on Conduit and revisit — no forced switch.
