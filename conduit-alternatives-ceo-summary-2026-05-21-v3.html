§01 The Call

Pick Duve. Pilot on Poem at cutover, then roll across the portfolio.

Recommendation
One vendor for all ~100 units across both lines: Duve. Pilot on Edificio Poem starting w/c 25 May (first VR cutover anyway), give the team four weeks of real-world use, sign the contract at day 30 if KPIs hold, then roll Duve onto each subsequent property as it migrates to Mews. Akia stays in reserve as the fall-back if the Poem pilot disappoints.

Two pieces of context shape this brief. First: Conduit was never actually implemented — we negotiated, the price came in too high, we walked away. So this isn't a Conduit-replacement exercise. It's a from-scratch procurement to backfill the unified inbox we lose when Guesty's PMS goes away. Second: yesterday's v2 brief recommended a hotel/VR split based on stale Guesty channel data for Masanet and Emeté. Those hotels are already on Mews — their live Airbnb and Booking connections moved with them, and Guesty no longer shows the truth. With all four hotels distributing on the full OTA stack, the cleanest move is one vendor for everyone.

§02 What v2 Got Wrong

Stale data, masquerading as ground truth.

v2 leaned on a per-property channel-mix query against Guesty to justify keeping Masanet & Emeté on Mews-native messaging. That query missed the obvious: once a property migrates to Mews, the live channel connections move with it. The Guesty rows I read were legacy artifacts, not the current state.

  1. The Guesty MT* listings showed only Expedia + Google because they're deprecated.
    Hotel Masanet and Hotel Emeté went live on Mews earlier this month. Mews is where the production Booking.com, Airbnb, and Expedia connections now run. The Guesty channel field for those listings only reflects what Guesty itself still has wired up — not the truth on the ground.
  2. Boutique hotels in leisure markets do use Airbnb. The "no" assumption was speculation.
    See §03 for the evidence. Short version: Airbnb is now actively recruiting boutique hotels, the channel is single-digit % of an Airbnb-side total but growing 2× the platform rate, and Gran Canaria specifically sits in Airbnb's strong leisure footprint. There was never a defensible reason to exclude our hotels.
  3. The split-strategy saved €0 once both hotels actually need OTA inbox.
    v2's headline number ("€450–700/mo across 89 units") assumed ~9 hotel rooms paid €0/mo. With those rooms back in scope the math shifts to ~100 units. The operational complexity of running two messaging stacks (one Mews-native for hotels, one third-party for VRs) disappears as a benefit — one vendor is also simpler for the team to onboard and maintain.
§03 Do Boutique Hotels Use Airbnb?

Yes — and the trend points sharply upward.

Booking.com is non-negotiable for any European hotel — that's the easy half. The harder question is whether Airbnb is worth wiring up for our four hotels. The 2025 industry data says yes, especially in our market.

"Airbnb is going significantly more aggressively into hotels," targeting bed-and-breakfasts, inns, and boutique hotels rather than large chains. Hotel-pilot programs launched October 2025 in New York, Los Angeles, and Madrid. — Brian Chesky, Airbnb Q4 2025 earnings call · summarised in RentalScaleUp

§07 The Ask

Three decisions before Poem goes live.

  1. Approve Duve as the OTA omninbox across all properties.
    One vendor, four hotels and every VR. No split-strategy, no Mews-native carve-out. If the Poem pilot uncovers a deal-breaker, Akia is the fall-back and we run a second 30-day pilot against it before committing. Both vendor pipelines stay warm during the Poem pilot so the fall-back move is fast.
  2. Authorise a 30-day Duve pilot on Edificio Poem starting w/c 25 May.
    Poem is the next migration anyway — the pilot rides on the cutover work, no extra coordination cost. Measure: OTA-message coverage across Airbnb + Booking + Expedia actually arriving, Canarias-Spanish reply quality, staff adoption from the Colombia ops team, response latency. Day 30: sign the Duve contract for the whole portfolio if KPIs hold, or pivot to Akia for a second pilot if they don't.
  3. Roll Duve onto each property at its own cutover.
    Bandama → Emeté → Eleven → Vegueta → BlueBalcony → Casa Vivar. One property per week, same as the Mews migration sequence. Each cutover already involves channel reconnection — folding Duve onboarding into that touch point means zero additional onboarding events for the team.
If you want to de-risk further: a parallel 30-day pilot of Duve and Akia on Poem would let us see both in action against the same property and same staff, picking the winner on data rather than reputation. The trade-off is real: it would mean two vendor onboardings simultaneously, two sets of staff training, two integrations to monitor — meaningfully more for our Colombia ops team to absorb in the same week as a property cutover. My recommendation is the sequential approach above; the dual pilot is the right answer only if Javier or Anna want maximum pricing leverage and we have the bandwidth to spare.
