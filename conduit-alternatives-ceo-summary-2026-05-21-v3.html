§01 The Call

All ~100 units, one OTA-inbox vendor. Pilot two, commit to one.

Recommendation
Every Airnest property — four hotels plus five VR buildings plus scattered VRs — needs the same unified OTA inbox. Run a 60-day dual pilot of Duve and Akia on Bandama Hotel + Poem, two of our highest-OTA-volume properties. Kill the loser on day 60, roll the winner across the portfolio, give Conduit 30-day notice on pilot day 45.

Yesterday's v2 brief recommended a hotel/VR split based on Guesty channel data showing Hotel Masanet and Hotel Emeté on Expedia + Google only. That data was stale — those hotels were already migrated to Mews, so the real OTA mix (which includes Airbnb and Booking.com) lives in Mews now, not Guesty. With all four hotels distributing on the full OTA stack — and industry data confirming this is the right posture for boutique hotels in leisure markets — the cleanest move is one vendor for everyone.

§02 What v2 Got Wrong

Stale data, masquerading as ground truth.

v2 leaned on a per-property channel-mix query against Guesty to justify keeping Masanet & Emeté on Mews-native messaging. That query missed the obvious: once a property migrates to Mews, the live channel connections move with it. The Guesty rows I read were legacy artifacts, not the current state.

  1. The Guesty MT* listings showed only Expedia + Google because they're deprecated.
    Hotel Masanet and Hotel Emeté went live on Mews earlier this month. Mews is where the production Booking.com, Airbnb, and Expedia connections now run. The Guesty channel field for those listings only reflects what Guesty itself still has wired up — not the truth on the ground.
  2. Boutique hotels in leisure markets do use Airbnb. The "no" assumption was speculation.
    See §03 for the evidence. Short version: Airbnb is now actively recruiting boutique hotels, the channel is single-digit % of an Airbnb-side total but growing 2× the platform rate, and Gran Canaria specifically sits in Airbnb's strong leisure footprint. There was never a defensible reason to exclude our hotels.
  3. The split-strategy saved €0 once both hotels actually need OTA inbox.
    v2's headline number ("€450–700/mo across 89 units") assumed ~9 hotel rooms paid €0/mo. With those rooms back in scope the math shifts to ~100 units — still cheaper than Conduit, but the savings shrink and the operational complexity of running two messaging stacks (one Mews-native for hotels, one third-party for VRs) disappears as a benefit.
§03 Do Boutique Hotels Use Airbnb?

Yes — and the trend points sharply upward.

Booking.com is non-negotiable for any European hotel — that's the easy half. The harder question is whether Airbnb is worth wiring up for our four hotels. The 2025 industry data says yes, especially in our market.

"Airbnb is going significantly more aggressively into hotels," targeting bed-and-breakfasts, inns, and boutique hotels rather than large chains. Hotel-pilot programs launched October 2025 in New York, Los Angeles, and Madrid. — Brian Chesky, Airbnb Q4 2025 earnings call · summarised in RentalScaleUp

Conclusion: none of our four hotels (Masanet, Veintiuno, Bandama, Emeté) should be off Airbnb in 2026. Whatever messaging stack we pick has to handle Airbnb inbound chat for the hotels just as it does for the VRs — the previously assumed split doesn't exist in the market.

The real prize isn't cost. Conduit is a 2.5% drag on 2026 EBITDA — meaningful, not catastrophic. The bigger reasons to switch are: (a) Mews-native depth from a vendor that integrates properly, (b) AI capabilities none of the current Conduit configuration uses, (c) avoiding a 12-month renewal lock-in at the same flat fee. Treat the €100–400/mo as the floor benefit, not the ceiling.

§07 The Ask

Three decisions before Poem goes live.

  1. Approve all-properties scope.
    Whatever vendor wins the pilot serves every hotel and every VR. No more split-strategy, no Mews-native carve-out for the hotels. This is the v3 correction.
  2. Authorise a 60-day dual pilot: Duve + Akia, on Bandama Hotel + Poem.
    Bandama because it's our highest-OTA-volume hotel and a real test of the inbox under hotel-style booking patterns. Poem because it's the next migration anyway and a fair VR-side benchmark. Total pilot spend < €1,500. Measure: OTA-message coverage across all three channels actually arriving, Canarias-Spanish reply quality, staff adoption, response latency vs the Conduit baseline. Day 60: kill loser, commit winner.
  3. Give Conduit 30-day notice at pilot day 45, not before.
    If both pilots fail (unlikely but possible) we stay on Conduit — don't burn the bridge prematurely. Day-45 timing means we exit Conduit roughly at day 75 of the pilot, with the winner already wired in for the rest of the portfolio.
