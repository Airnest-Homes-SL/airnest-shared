Replacing Conduit for unified guest messaging on Mews — vendor scan and recommendation
From Luis Navarro · 20 May 2026 · Internal · 5-min read For Javier — decision needed before Poem cutover (w/c 25 May)
§01 The Call

Drop Conduit. Pilot HelloShift.

Recommendation
Move OTA + WhatsApp + SMS messaging to HelloShift at the Mews cutover. ≈€315/mo vs Conduit's ≈€900/mo — ≈€7,000/yr saved. Run a 60-day overlap on Poem + Bandama, then switch the rest of the portfolio.

Conduit costs ≈€900/mo for 137 listings (~€10.8k/yr). Three Mews-native platforms deliver the same unified-inbox job — OTA messaging, WhatsApp, SMS, email — at materially lower cost. The best fit for our portfolio shape (many small units, lean team, no need for enterprise AI) is HelloShift at roughly one-third Conduit's price. Bookboost and Trengo are the runners-up if HelloShift's AI proves too light in pilot.

§02 Why Move Now

The Mews cutover is the moment.

Conduit was the obvious choice on Guesty because of the native integration. On Mews, that lock-in is gone — we have to re-point Conduit anyway, so the switching cost to a cheaper vendor is the same switching cost. If we don't decide now, we'll pay Conduit prices by default for another year.

  1. Conduit's €900/mo is a 3% drag on 2026 EBITDA.
    On the 12% EBITDA target against €3.6M booking sales (≈€432k), €10.8k/yr in messaging fees is recoverable margin — not catastrophic, but it compounds against every other vendor line we don't push back on.
  2. Poem cutover is the natural switch point.
    Guest-comms is already flagged as the blocker for w/c 25 May. Whatever we choose has to be wired up before Poem goes live. Defaulting to "re-point Conduit" locks in the price for another renewal cycle.
  3. The market caught up to Conduit on features.
    Mews integration, OTA aggregation, WhatsApp, AI suggested replies — all available at €2–4 per unit/mo from multiple vendors. Conduit's edge was packaging, not capability.

§04 Why HelloShift Wins

Cheapest per room that still does the full job.

A · Coverage
SMS + WhatsApp + FB Messenger + email
Unified inbox across the channels we actually use. Mews data syncs automatically — staff see reservation + guest context next to each conversation. OTA messaging via the Mews-Booking.com/Airbnb pipes covers the rest.
B · Operations
Built-in housekeeping & maintenance
Includes staff collaboration, task assignment, and housekeeping/maintenance modules at the same price. Adjacent to AOS — same workflow surface our Colombia team already lives in. No extra tool sprawl.
C · Economics
Linear per-room price
$1.25/room Starter + $1.25/room Guest+ = ~€2.30/room. No minimum-property fee, no setup fee, free trial. The price scales cleanly from 137 → 180 units without renegotiation.

The trade-off: HelloShift's AI is "suggested replies + snippets," not a full conversational agent. If 2027's voice-AI build subsumes guest-messaging, HelloShift becomes a feeder — that's a feature, not a bug. We don't want to over-invest in vendor AI we'll cannibalise with our own AOS module.

§06 What I'm Asking

Three decisions before Poem goes live.

  1. Greenlight a 60-day HelloShift pilot on Poem + Bandama.
    Free trial → Starter+Guest+ at ~€5/mo for the two pilot properties. We measure: OTA-message coverage, response latency vs Conduit baseline, staff adoption, AI-reply quality in Canarias-Spanish. Clean exit at day 60.
  2. Authorise me to give Conduit 30-day notice once Poem is live and stable.
    If pilot KPIs hold, I cancel Conduit at the next monthly cycle and migrate the rest of the portfolio in week 3 of the pilot. If KPIs miss, we pivot to Bookboost or Trengo within the same 60-day window. Either way, no second renewal.
  3. Confirm: vendor AI stays "good enough" — we build the differentiated layer ourselves.
    Same logic as the voice-AI brief: don't pay enterprise prices for vendor AI we'll cannibalise with the 2027 AOS/ElevenLabs build. HelloShift's lighter AI is the right level for a 2026 commodity tool.
