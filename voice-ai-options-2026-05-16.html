§01 Before the options — a discipline note

Don't open another front while so many are still mid-build.

Why this framing matters

We are already running a stack of heavy onboardings in parallel: the Guesty → Mews migration (8 properties, 7 still kicking off), the Mews API Connector build, the Ops Brain / AOS rollout, Flexkeeping, SiteMinder, and the new boutique hotels coming online under Airnest Hotels. Every one of those is itself a "new onboarding project" — each demands attention, integration work, training, and team bandwidth.

Adding a voice-AI build or a full 12-month vendor contract on top of that is not a free option — even at $33k/yr, the real cost is the focus we pull away from work that is already 60–80% done. The cheapest, fastest path to value is to finish what we started, and only then expand the surface.

§02 The four real options

A, B, C, D — what each path actually buys.

A
Hire one more Colombia rep, defer the AI decision to Q4 2026
Luis preferred
  • +$12k/yr team cost (3 → 4 people), $0 AI spend
  • Carries us through 180 units comfortably
  • We finish Mews_API_Connector and Ops Brain on schedule
  • Q4 2026 we scope voice AI on top of a mature Mews connector
  • Launch v1 in Q1–Q2 2027 with a proper foundation
Risk: discipline. "Defer" can become "never." Needs a hard re-decision date on the calendar.
B
Conduit 90-day pilot (push back on the 12-month term)
Fallback
  • Negotiate $3–4k/month × 3 months ≈ $10k total, clean exit
  • Treat it as a paid intelligence-gathering exercise: real call transcripts, real deflection rates, hospitality-tuned prompts, Canarias-Spanish voice quality benchmark
  • We don't touch code; we stay focused on the Mews connector
  • At day 75: renew, kill, or migrate
Risk: Salesbricks may refuse a 3-month term. Worth asking before assuming.
C
Conduit 12-month + parallel build (Javier's "lean," with an explicit endgame)
AI co-recommend
  • Year 1: Conduit at $33k. We stay focused on the Mews connector through Q4 2026
  • Q1 2027: start building the ElevenLabs replacement on top of the finished Mews connector, informed by Conduit's data
  • At renewal: switch. Save $20–30k/yr from there forward
Risk: the switch never actually happens (sunk cost / inertia). The endgame date has to be written down, not implied.
D
Fully autonomous AI + a single on-call emergency contact
2028+ endgame
  • AI handles 100% of inbound and structured outbound
  • One on-call human is the legal 24/7 contact, paged only for emergencies
  • Colombia team drops from 3 → 1 part-time → ~$24–30k/yr saved
  • Legal: likely OK for Airnest Collection under Decreto 113/2015 (reachable, not staffed). Not OK as-is for Airnest Hotels — boutique hotel category likely requires staffed/24-7 reception. Needs a lawyer's read before we bet on it.
Risk: a single bad AI interaction during a real incident (broken AC in August, locked-out guest at midnight) = refund + bad review + possible regulator complaint. Plus the HR/structural reality of winding down headcount inside a family business. This is an endgame, not a 2026 plan.
§03 Where we land

Two reads of the same picture.

Luis's preference
Start with A. Step into A + C over the next 3 months. D is the endgame.

Now → next 3 months: Option A — hire one more Colombia rep. That single move absorbs 2026 growth without opening another front, and it lets the team finish what's already in flight: the Mews migration on the 7 remaining properties, Flexkeeping, SiteMinder, the new hotels, the Ops Brain build, and the Mews API Connector.

Then, gradually → A + C: once the Mews connector is mature enough to be the substrate (Conduit would depend on it anyway), step into C with eyes open — bring Conduit in as a paid intelligence layer, intake its real-world call data, and start scoping the eventual in-house build informed by that data.

Endgame → D: fully autonomous + a single on-call contact stays on the board as the 2028+ north star, gated on the legal read for Airnest Hotels.

AI analysis
Same sequence — A now, A + C as the connector matures, D as endgame.
The phased approach is the right one. A alone protects the current onboarding stack and the connector schedule. Layering C in 2–3 months later — once the Mews connector is past its critical path — turns Conduit into a data feed for our own future build rather than a permanent dependency. By the time renewal hits, we have: (a) the connector finished, (b) a year of real hospitality call data, and (c) a credible in-house path. D becomes a 2028 decision, not a 2026 bet.

The two reads converge: hire the rep now, finish the current onboarding stack, then bring Conduit in as a data layer on top of a mature connector, with D as the long-term destination. The 30-minute conversation is no longer "if" — it's when we step from A into A + C, and what the trigger is.
